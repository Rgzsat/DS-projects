This thesis focuses on enhancing the performance and reliability
of Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) through a multidisciplinar
methodology that combines expertise from Electrical Engineering, Mechatronics,
Computer Science, Software Engineering, Energy Engineering, and Data Engineering.
The central research question is: How can the integration of advanced methodologies
from diverse technical fields improve the resilience and operational efficiency of
both physical and virtual models of a BESS?

The research design includes experimental battery testing, 
hybrid model development, and the implementation of software
infrastructure to address operational challenges in both physical systems and virtual environments. 
Additionally, this thesis bridges gaps between academia and industry by integrating algorithmic 
approaches from AI and energy systems.
