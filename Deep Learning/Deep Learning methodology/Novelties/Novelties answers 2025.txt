This thesis focuses on enhancing the performance and reliability
of Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) through a multidisciplinar
methodology that combines expertise from Electrical Engineering, Mechatronics,
Computer Science, Software Engineering, Energy Engineering, and Data Engineering.
The central research question is: How can the integration of advanced methodologies
from diverse technical fields improve the resilience and operational efficiency of
both physical and virtual models of a BESS?

The research design includes experimental battery testing, 
hybrid model development, and the implementation of software
infrastructure to address operational challenges in both physical systems and virtual environments. 
Additionally, this thesis bridges gaps between academia and industry by integrating algorithmic 
approaches from AI and energy systems.

The main objectives of the thesis are:
•	To develop a robust and adaptable framework for optimizing BESS performance,
considering both physical degradation and virtual modeling limitations such as
concept and data drift.
•	To support the creation of resilient energy storage systems that contribute 
to the EU’s goals for sustainable energy and climate change mitigation.
•	To implement APIs and relational databases to improve data management, 
coding efficiency, and system responsiveness to customer demands such as state estimation, 
Remaining Useful Lifetime (RUL), and charging management.

Research question
In the context of the global energy transition, energy technologies have taken
center stage across virtually all sectors. Among these, a 
Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) has emerged as key enablers for renewable
energy integration, grid stabilization, and sustainable energy supply. 
This thesis explores a comprehensive and interdisciplinary approach to enhancing the design,
monitoring, and operation of BESS, focusing on their technological optimization 
through advanced modeling, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and software engineering. 
The central research question guiding this work is: How can multidisciplinary and
AI-driven methods be integrated to enhance the performance, reliability, and 
adaptability of Battery Energy Storage Systems in real-world environments?
